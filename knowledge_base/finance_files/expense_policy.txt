Expense Policy

Purpose:
This policy describes what expenses are reimbursable, the approval workflow, and documentation requirements.

Submission and Timing:
- Expense reports should be submitted within 30 days of the expense date.
- Expenses submitted after 60 days require manager justification and may be declined.

Reimbursable Expenses:
- Travel (airfare, train, public transport) when pre-approved for business purposes.
- Accommodation for overnight business travel at reasonable rates.
- Meals while traveling for business or during approved client entertainment.
- Office supplies and small equipment purchases approved by your manager.

Non-Reimbursable Expenses:
- Personal expenses, family travel, fines (parking, traffic), and alcohol not associated with approved
	client entertainment.

Approval and Documentation:
- All expense items require original receipts or electronic equivalents.
- Expenses above $500 require manager and finance approval before reimbursement.
- Use the corporate expense system and attach receipts; company card transactions should be reconciled
	on the monthly statement.

Per Diem and Currency:
- Per-diem rules apply for multi-day travel; check the travel appendix for regional rates.
- For foreign currency expenses, submit receipts and the amount in local currency; finance will use the
	company exchange rate on the date of the transaction.

Audit and Record Retention:
- Finance may audit expense reports; retain receipts for 6 months after submission.

Contact:
Submit questions to finance@company.example or consult the Finance section on the intranet.
